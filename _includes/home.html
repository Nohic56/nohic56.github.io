PhD Title:

New Quantification Approaches of Proteome Variations at the Intact Proteins Level: Experimental and Computational Analyses of Pathological Conditions in Humans.

My PhD research was conducted at the:

Institut Jacques Monod

Abstract:

One of the most challenging problems in modern biology is analyzing proteome variations in terms of composition and structure. This is crucial for understanding the mechanisms of gene expression and regulation under normal and pathological conditions. In mass spectrometry (MS)-based proteomics, the Simple Light Isotope Metabolic (SLIM) labeling technique has proven to be a highly effective tool for deeply analyzing complex proteomes, both in bottom-up and top-down experiments.

The main objectives of my PhD were:
1) To develop robust statistical approaches for analyzing SLIM-based quantitative proteomics data.
2) To apply the SLIM strategy to analyze proteoform variations in pathogenic yeast (Candida albicans) as a model organism, in order to establish systems biology approaches.
3) To extend these methods to more complex proteomes from higher eukaryotic cells.

The techniques developed during my PhD were applied to analyze pathological conditions in humans, focusing on neurodegenerative disorders like Friedreich Ataxia, where phenotypic expression is highly dependent on genomic structure.

Keywords:

Proteomics, Data Processing, Computational and Systems Biology, Biostatistics, Cell Culture, Biochemistry
